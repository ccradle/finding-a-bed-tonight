## For Shelter Operators Who Want to Start Simple

### You Don't Have to Use Everything

FABT has many features — bed search, reservations, DV referrals, surge events, analytics, HMIS push. **You don't have to use any of them except bed count updates.** Every shelter can participate at the level that works for their operations and capacity.

### Participation Levels

#### Level 1: Bed Count Updates Only

**What you do:** Log in once or twice a day. Update how many beds are occupied. Log out.

**What you don't do:** No reservations, no referrals, no surge events, no analytics.

**Who this is for:** Volunteer-run shelters, seasonal shelters (White Flag nights), faith community shelters with minimal staff.

**How it works:**
1. Your CoC admin creates your shelter profile and a coordinator account
2. You log in via browser (phone or computer)
3. Find your shelter in the coordinator dashboard
4. Update the occupied count using +/- buttons
5. Press "Save" — you see confirmation
6. Done. Total time: under 30 seconds.

**What outreach workers see:** Your shelter appears in search results with the availability you reported. They can call your shelter directly — they don't need to hold beds through the app.

#### Level 2: Bed Counts + Reservations

**What you add:** Outreach workers can hold a bed at your shelter for 45 minutes while transporting a client.

**What this means for you:** You see a "Holds" count on your dashboard. These auto-expire after 45 minutes if no one arrives. You don't need to do anything — the system manages it.

**Who this is for:** Shelters with reliable nightly staffing who want to reduce phone calls from outreach workers.

#### Level 3: Full Participation

**What you add:** DV referrals (if applicable), surge events, analytics.

**Who this is for:** Professional shelter operations with dedicated staff and a CoC admin managing the system.

### Seasonal Shelters

**Q: We only operate during cold weather. What happens during our off-season?**

Nothing. Your shelter profile stays in the system but shows zero available beds. No cost during idle months (the hosting fee is the same regardless of activity). When cold weather returns, a coordinator logs in, updates the bed count, and your shelter appears in search results again.

**Q: Can we mark ourselves as temporarily closed?**

Set all bed counts to zero and set "accepting new guests" to No. Your shelter will appear in search results as "Currently Full" (not accepting). When you reopen, update the count.

### Getting Started

1. Ask your CoC administrator to create a shelter profile for you
2. They will create a coordinator account (email + password)
3. Log in on any device with a browser — no app install required
4. Update your bed count whenever occupancy changes

**Time to learn:** Under 10 minutes. The coordinator dashboard is designed for 3-5 taps per update.

**Training required:** None beyond this guide. If your coordinator can use a smartphone, they can use FABT.

### What You're Not Committing To

- No contract or agreement to sign
- No minimum update frequency (though more frequent = more useful for outreach workers)
- No requirement to use reservations, referrals, or any advanced features
- No cost to your organization (the CoC or city pays the hosting)
- No data you need to provide beyond bed counts
